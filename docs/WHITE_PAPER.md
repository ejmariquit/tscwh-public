# TSCWH White Paper (Public Summary)

## Executive Summary

TSCWH (The Stone Cut Without Hands) is a covenant enforcement framework
for AI systems. It is designed to evaluate and validate proposed AI actions
against ethical covenants in real time, ensuring that machine behavior aligns
with human-defined moral principles.

The core innovation is the combination of multi-agent deliberation with a
comprehensive safety stack. Rather than relying on static rule lists or
post-hoc classifiers, TSCWH engages ten independent agents to debate each
action and applies multiple safety mechanisms to monitor integrity,
performance, and adversarial resilience.

## The Challenge

Existing AI safety solutions often fall short: overly rigid filters impede
legitimate use, while permissive classifiers can be bypassed. Static rules are
easily evaded by adversaries and cannot adapt to evolving contexts or
ethical norms.

## TSCWH Approach

- **Dialectical council:** Ten agents provide diverse perspectives (e.g.
  guardian, advocate, predictor, sentinel) resulting in balanced decisions.
- **Layered safety:** Twenty-five mechanisms such as cryptographic
  signing, anomaly detection, kill-switch levels, and self-monitoring
  work in concert to provide defense in depth.
- **Adaptive governance:** Mathematical stabilizers regulate confidence and
  mercy to prevent drift and exploitation.
- **Human‑in‑the‑loop:** When the system is uncertain, it suspends action and
  defers to human judgment (a concept called SABBATH state).

## Capabilities

- Operates as a lightweight middleware with low latency (<100 ms per
  evaluation).
- Detects jailbreaks and prompt injections using pattern-based analysis.
- Maintains a low false positive rate on legitimate actions.
- Supports five graduated emergency shutdown levels with automatic escalation.

## Use Cases

- Enterprises integrating AI into customer-facing products requiring
  ethical oversight.
- Research organizations studying safe self-modifying systems.
- Any deployment where AI actions must be auditable and constrained by
  explicit covenants.
